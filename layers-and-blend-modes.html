The Layers Panel

The layers panel is something that differentiates digital painting from traditional painting. With layers, you can paint over a layer while still being able to change it later on. You can also create a new layer to experiment, and if you don't like what you did, you can completely scrap it without affecting the rest of your painting.

Unless you plan on painting everything on one layer, you should get to know the layers panel. This panel is found on the bottom right corner of the screen.

When you create a document, you will have 2 layers labeled "Paint Layer 1" and "Background." The background layer is locked by default so that you don't accidentally erase the background when you just try to erase your lines.

Blend Modes

Blend modes can be difficult to understand, and they are best learned by experimenting. Essentially, blend modes change the way colors look depending on the colors of the layer below it.

At the top of the layers panel is a dropdown menu that allows you to change the blend mode of the selected layer. Blend modes are a little complicated, but they essentially change how the colors on the layer appears based on the colors on the layer(s) below it.

You can learn more about each blend mode on the official Krita documentation website by clicking here, but the two main ones that I use for digital painting are overlay and multiply. As shown in the painting demo, I use overlay to lighten up the layers below it to paint light, and I use multiply to darken the layers below it to create shadows.

I encourage you to play around with each blend mode to see what they do to the colors on a layer.
