Brushes

When painting, you will be using the brush tool for the majority of the time. In the toolbar on the left side of the screen, the brush tool is indicated by the paintbrush icon. The shortcut for the brush tool is B on your keyboard.

At the top of your screen, there are 5 important buttons/settings to be aware of. These are:

  1. Edit brush settings
  2. Choose a brush preset
  3. Toggle on/off the eraser
  4. Change the opacity of your brush/eraser
  5. Change the size of your brush/eraser

I usually do not touch these buttons unless I am switching brushes or changing the opacity. Using shortcuts for changing the brush size and switching to and from the eraser is much more efficient.

Brush Settings

The first button is the "Edit brush settings" button. Note that you must have the brush tool selected to press this button. This menu allows you to change things like your brush's size, angle, shape, and much more.

I suggest checking the checkbox at the bottom left that says "Eraser switch size." With this option selected, whenever you are using this brush and you switch to the eraser, it will remember the size of the eraser when you last used it. For example, if you change the size of your eraser and then switch back to the brush, the brush's size will not be the same as the eraser's. This is helpful if you want your eraser to stay the same size as when you last used it. You can also check "Eraser switch opacity" if you want the same thing but with the opacity setting.

Nothing else in this menu is necessary to learn. You can get a gist of what each setting does by playing around with each setting and testing the brush out on the scratchpad to the right. You can learn more about each setting on the official Krita documentation website here.

Brush Presets

By default, Krita comes with a variety of brushes and erasers for you to try. There is everything from basic round brushes to pencils to brushes that simulate real paintbrushes, to brushes that produce special effects.

You can experiment with all of the brushes, but I recommend starting out with the basic opacity brush and basic eraser circle, which is what I use 99% of the time.
